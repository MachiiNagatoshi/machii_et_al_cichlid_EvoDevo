# machii_et_al_cichlid_EvoDevo

Title:Mosaic of evolutionary origins underlies parallel evolution of hypertrophied lips in East African cichlids

Abstract
Parallel evolution provides a powerful framework for understanding the genetic basis of adaptation. Whether parallel phenotypes arise through independent mutations or shared variation via standing genetic variation or hybridization, remains a central question particularly for polygenic traits. Here, we investigate the genetic and developmental basis of hypertrophied lips, one of the representative examples of trophic adaptation and parallel evolution across cichlid fishes in the three East African Great Lakes. We integrated genome-wide scans, time-series transcriptomics and single cell transcriptomics in Lake Victoria cichlids, and demonstrates that multiple genes under natural selection drive the formation of hypertrophied lips, including Wnt signaling genes, collagen XII genes, and glycosylation-related genes. In addition, whole genome phylogenetic analyses indicate that glycosylation-related loci share haplotypes across lakes, which are hallmarks of ancient standing genetic variation maintained for more than 2.8 million years. Our results show that the genetic architecture of a parallel polygenic trait is assembled from loci with different evolutionary origins. This mosaic architecture sheds light on a comprehensive understanding of how complex phenotypes evolve repeatedly from diverse evolutionary histories.
